# 01 · Brand Strategy

## 1.1 The one-liner

> **DayBeans is the one quiet page that knows what kind of day you need to face.**

## 1.2 Brand mission, vision, promise

| | |
|---|---|
| **Mission** | Help anyone — mindful, spiritual, secular, "just here for the coffee" — start each morning with one quiet, curated page that bends to who they are. |
| **Vision** | Replace the noisy 5-tabs-and-a-doomscroll morning with a single, calm, custom brief — and tie it to small daily wins (and free coffee). |

## 1.3 What "DayBeans" means

A bean is a small, packed unit of potential. So is a day. We say "bean" because:

1. **Coffee** is the universal morning ritual — secular, religious, introvert, extrovert, type-A, type-Zzz. It crosses every audience we want.
2. **Beans accumulate** — perfect mental model for streaks, goals, and "bean count" progress.
3. **It's witty without being clever-clever.** Easy to say, easy to remember, hard to misuse.
4. **It scales to a partnership story** (Starbucks, Caribou, Scooter's, local roasters) — see [04 epic 7](04-product-requirements.md#epic-7--bean-streaks--coffee-rewards).

> Boba folks: please report to a different cup. We're not into *that* bean.

## 1.4 Audience

DayBeans is built for the **morning-curious adult** — someone who already wants to start
their day with intention but is tired of the tools that demand a lifestyle in return.

| **Marcus · Master carpenter, 45** | 14-yr trade, weekend Bible study, three kids, woodworking blog he never updates. | Wants curated craft news, not generic "tech tips". Wants Sunday verse and weekday hustle without code-switching apps. |

## 1.5 Voice & tone

### The voice has three settings, and the product knows which to use.

| Setting | Where it shows up | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Quiet** | Inside the product. Pour Over verses, journal nudges, small UI copy. | "Today, before you respond to anything, breathe twice and ask one question." |
| **Warm-witty** | Marketing surfaces, onboarding, hero copy, error states. | "Different beans. Same morning." · "We're not bat-bean crazy." |
| **Direct** | Settings, billing, privacy, exports. | "Your journal stays on your device. Export anytime as JSON." |

### Voice rules

1. **Be witty without being clever.** A pun should land in one read. If we're explaining the joke, cut it.
2. **Coffee metaphors are flavor, not the meal.** One per surface, max. Don't rename "save" to "pour."
3. **Never preach.** We carry verse for those who want it; we don't argue scripture is true. We don't argue it isn't.
4. **No emojis in product copy.** They cheapen the calm. Marketing surfaces can use one or two with restraint.
5. **No fake urgency.** No countdown timers. No "act now." Mornings are not the place for that energy.
6. **One sentence is almost always enough.**
7. **Weave faith and secular options into the same paragraph.** Both audiences should feel addressed at once. Example: *"Some people pray. Some stretch. Some pull a Chemex. All welcome."*

### Words we use

> brew · pour · sip · steep · grind · linger · gather · tend · quiet · today · whatever your bean

### Words we don't

> hustle · grind (when it means "work hard") · disrupt · level up · 10x · journey *(unless we mean the verb)* · vibe · slay · era

## 1.6 Brand pillars

Five pillars that every product, design, and copy decision should ladder up to.

### 1. **Quiet by default**
One tab. No notifications by default. No popovers. No "are you sure?" If a feature shouts, it doesn't ship.

### 2. **Curated, not crammed**
The home page is a brief, not a feed. Every morning we hide more than we show. The signal is what's missing.

### 3. **Custom, not personalized**
"Personalized" is what algorithms do. "Custom" is what tailors do. Our content is sewn — it bends around your job title, hobbies, faith preference, and the journal you wrote last night.

### 4. **Kind to everyone's bean**
Faith-aware without being faith-required. Secular-friendly without being faith-hostile. Themes for goth and grandma. Boba folks gently redirected.

### 5. **Small wins, real rewards**
Streaks pay off in actual coffee — not metaphors, not points, not badges. Bean Count is the lighthearted scoreboard, never a guilt machine.

## 1.8 The coffee-as-marketing strategy

Coffee isn't just a metaphor — it's the **partnership and rewards engine**.

### Phase 1 · Indie roasters (months 0–3)
- Partner with **3–5 local roasters per launch city** (Omaha, Austin, Portland, Brooklyn, Seattle).
- Trade: featured "Roaster of the Week" placement in the app footer + landing page → roaster gives 100 single-cup vouchers per week to DayBeans.
- We hand the vouchers out to users hitting **7-morning streaks**.

### Phase 2 · Chain partners (months 3–9)
- Approach **Caribou, Scooter's, Peet's, Blue Bottle**. Caribou + Scooter's first — they're hungrier for app integrations than Starbucks.
- Trade: co-branded "Bean Streak Bundle" — 7-morning streak earns a $5 mobile-order credit. They get reactivation; we get retention.
- Starbucks last (highest bar, biggest payoff). Likely needs a year of metrics behind it.

### Phase 3 · Beans-by-mail subscription (months 9–18)
- DayBeans-branded **monthly bean drop** featuring a different roaster's micro-lot, mailed to active monthly users.
- Optional add-on, $15/mo. Becomes a margin product.

### Tone of partner copy
- **Not** "Sponsored by Caribou."
- **Yes** "Today's free pour, courtesy of Caribou. Their drip is doing its job."

## 1.9 Pricing & business model (brand-shaped)

| Tier | Price | What you get | Why it's brand-on |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First Pour** | Free | Daily content (limited LLM use), 1 theme, basic journal, weekly streak | Lowers the bar; reinforces "quiet by default" |
| **Whole Bean** | $7/mo or $60/yr | Full curation, all 15 themes + custom wallpaper, bookmarks, drawer journal, hobby tracking, coffee streak rewards | "Whole bean" matches the brand language; price under a fancy latte/wk |
| **Roaster** | $15/mo or $120/yr | Whole Bean + monthly DayBeans bean-drop in the mail | Physical product becomes the loyalty driver |
| **Bring-your-own-key** | Free → $3/mo | Use your own LLM key. Discounted because we don't pay inference. | Power users; honest pricing |

## 1.10 Anti-brand list — what we won't do

- We won't add a "social feed" or "community" tab. Too noisy.
- We won't gamify with badges, points, or rank ladders. Streaks pay in coffee, period.
- We won't push notifications by default. They opt in to a single morning email, max.
- We won't ever sell or surface aggregated journal content. The journal stays on the user's device until they export.
- We won't run interstitial ads. Sponsored coffee is the only paid surface, and it looks editorial, not banner-ish.
- We won't pivot to "DayBeans for teams." That's a different product.
